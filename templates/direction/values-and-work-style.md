# Values & Work Style

What you actually need from a job to do your best work, and what drains you.

## How You Work Best
> Where and how you're most productive (remote, office, hybrid, hours, environment, focus blocks, deep work patterns).
> Async vs. sync — when you need each.
> Time structuring habits.
> Tools and systems you rely on.

## What You Value in a Workplace
> Clarity of leadership direction. Purpose. Autonomy. Mastery. Compensation. Work-life balance. Team quality. Learning. Impact.
>
> Be honest about ranking — when these conflict, which wins? What does each one look like *concretely* for you (not the LinkedIn version)?

## What Drains You
> Specific things that consistently degrade your output or wellbeing in past roles. Be granular.
> Hard-but-rewarding vs. hard-and-miserable: what's the difference for you?

## Working With Others

### Leading Direct Reports
> If applicable. Your style. Failure modes you're aware of. Concrete signals it works (or doesn't).

### Working with Peers
> What you appreciate. What you find difficult. How you handle cross-team work.

### Managing Up
> Honest read on your strengths and weaknesses with managing up. This often connects to autonomy preferences in counterintuitive ways.

### What Kind of Manager You Want Above You
> Concrete attributes. What would make a manager work *for you* specifically?

## Non-Negotiables
> The shortest possible list of things that absolutely must be true. If something appears here it should also appear in `target-roles-and-criteria.md` under Hard Criteria.

## Open Questions
> Anything unresolved or contradictory.
