# Professional Identity & Narrative

Who you are, professionally — the version that holds up under interview pressure and adapts cleanly to different audiences.

## Positioning
> What's your one-line headline? (e.g. "Head of Product", "Staff Engineer focused on developer tooling")
> What's the longer one-liner — the version you'd write at the top of a CV or portfolio?
> Do you have a tagline you actually use? If so, what does it claim?
> How does your positioning shift depending on audience (e.g. startup vs. corporate, technical vs. business)?

## Career Narrative
> What is the through-line across your career? What problem do you keep coming back to?
> Why have you made the moves you've made? Walk me through the logic of the major transitions.
> What's the narrative you want to *avoid*? (e.g. "job hopper", "consultant who never shipped", "burned out senior")

## Identity as a [your role type]
> What kind of [PM / engineer / designer / leader] are you? (strategist, operator, builder, coach, hybrid?)
> What's your management or working philosophy in a few sentences?
> What do people who've worked with you say about you? (Actual quotes or recurring themes from feedback.)

## Differentiation
> What do you bring that's hard to find in the market?
> Where are you genuinely different from the typical person in your role?

## Open Questions
> Anything in this section that feels unresolved or that contradicts something else in the repo — list it here so the next interview pass can address it.
